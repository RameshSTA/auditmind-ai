Q1 2026 TRAVEL AND EXPENSE POLICY

Effective January 1, 2026. Supersedes the FY2025 policy in full.

1. GENERAL PRINCIPLE
All business travel and expense claims must be reasonable, necessary, and directly related to
Company business. Employees are expected to exercise the same care spending Company funds as they
would their own.

2. APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
- Expense reports under $500: approved by direct manager.
- Expense reports $500 to $2,500: approved by department head.
- Expense reports above $2,500: approved by department head and reviewed by Finance before
  reimbursement.
- Any single expense line item at or above $10,000 requires pre-approval from the Controller
  before the expense is incurred, not just before reimbursement.

3. VENDOR PAYMENTS VS. EMPLOYEE REIMBURSEMENT
Expenses billed directly by a vendor to the Company (e.g., a contracted facilities vendor invoicing
for services) are not employee expense reports and instead follow the standard accounts payable
purchase-order and invoice-matching process described in the Purchase Order Approval Matrix.

4. SPLIT TRANSACTIONS
Employees and approving managers must not split a single purchase into multiple smaller
transactions to stay under an approval threshold. Finance reviews transaction patterns quarterly
for round-dollar amounts clustered just below approval thresholds (for example, repeated charges
in the $9,500-$9,999 range against a $10,000 threshold) as a standard indicator warranting
follow-up, consistent with the Company's anti-fraud program.

5. RECEIPTS
Original itemized receipts are required for any expense over $75. Credit card statements alone are
not sufficient documentation.

6. CORPORATE CARD MISUSE
Personal use of a corporate card, even if reimbursed promptly, must be reported to Finance. Repeated
undisclosed personal use is grounds for disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Questions should be directed to the Finance Shared Services mailbox.

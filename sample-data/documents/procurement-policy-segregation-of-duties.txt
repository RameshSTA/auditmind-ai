PROCUREMENT POLICY — SEGREGATION OF DUTIES

Effective for all procurement and accounts payable activity, FY2026.

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the minimum segregation of duties required across the procure-to-pay
cycle to reduce the risk of error or fraud going undetected.

2. CORE SEGREGATION REQUIREMENTS
- The individual who requests a purchase may not also approve it.
- The individual who approves a purchase order may not also approve the corresponding invoice for
  payment.
- The individual who adds or modifies a vendor record in the vendor master file may not also
  approve payments to that vendor within the same reporting period without a compensating review.
- Accounts Payable staff who process payment runs may not also reconcile the accounts payable
  sub-ledger to the general ledger.

3. VENDOR MASTER FILE CHANGES
All additions, bank detail changes, and reactivations of previously inactive vendors require
two-person approval: one Procurement staff member to initiate the change and one Procurement
Manager or above to approve it. Bank detail changes additionally require a callback verification
to a phone number on file prior to the change (not a number provided in the change request itself).

4. EXCEPTIONS
Any exception to this policy must be documented, time-bound, and approved by the Controller. A
standing log of active exceptions is maintained by Internal Audit and reviewed quarterly.

5. MONITORING
Internal Audit performs a quarterly sample review of vendor master file changes and a quarterly
review of transactions exhibiting patterns associated with control override, including duplicate
payments, round-dollar amounts clustered near approval thresholds, and payments processed outside
normal business hours.

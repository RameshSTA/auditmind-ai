MEMO — RELATED PARTY DISCLOSURE REVIEW

To: Audit Committee
From: Corporate Controller
Date: January 20, 2026
Re: Q1 2026 related-party vendor disclosure review

This memo summarizes the annual related-party screening performed against the active vendor
master file ahead of the Q1 2026 close.

BACKGROUND
Company policy requires any vendor relationship where a Company officer, director, or their
immediate family holds a direct or indirect ownership interest of 5% or more to be disclosed to
the Audit Committee and flagged in the vendor master file.

SCREENING RESULTS
The vendor master file (142 active vendors as of this review) was cross-checked against the
officer and director disclosure questionnaires collected in December 2025. No new related-party
relationships were identified in this cycle. One previously disclosed relationship remains active:
a board member's spouse holds a minority, non-controlling interest in a marketing vendor engaged
by the Company; that engagement continues to be reviewed and re-approved annually by the Audit
Committee under the existing conflict-of-interest waiver.

RECOMMENDATION
No further action required for Q1 2026. The next full screening cycle is scheduled for Q4 2026,
concurrent with the annual disclosure questionnaire refresh. Any newly onboarded vendor during the
quarter should be checked against officer/director disclosures at the time of vendor setup, not
deferred to the next annual cycle.

Prepared by the Office of the Controller.
